Popular Tourist Destinations

Waterfalls

Riam Merasap

Riam Merasap is one of the most famous waterfalls (riam) in Bengkayang Regency, West Kalimantan, often nicknamed "Miniature Niagara" or "The Niagara of Bengkayang" because its wide shape resembles a curtain of water. The name Riam Merasap is derived from the unique phenomenon where the dense spray of water falling from a height of about 20 meters creates a cool mist or foam that looks like smoke.

Riam Berawan

Riam Berawan is a beautiful waterfall located in Bengkayang. Its name, which means "Cloudy Waterfall," comes from the mist that often hangs around its basin. The waterfall is surrounded by lush greenery, making it a perfect spot for nature lovers and adventurers. The clear, refreshing water cascades down multiple levels, creating a serene and picturesque environment for swimming and relaxation.

Riam Pangar

Riam Pangar is a charming aquatic ecotourism destination in Bengkayang Regency. The waterfall is located in Segonde Hamlet, Pisak Village, Tujuh Belas District, and its water source comes from the pristine Gunung Nyiut Nature Reserve.

The uniqueness of Riam Pangar lies in its three-tiered waterfall, with a total height of about 17 meters, and its very clear and strong current. Besides enjoying the beauty of the waterfall and swimming in its natural pools, Riam Pangar is also a popular location for adventure activities like white-water rafting, body rafting, and tubing, making it a major attraction for nature and adrenaline lovers.

Hills and Forests

Sepadang Hill

Sepadang Hill is a major highland destination in Bengkayang Regency that offers a hiking and camping experience with spectacular natural views.

The hill, at an altitude of about 500 meters above sea level (masl), is famous for its nickname "Land Above the Clouds" because visitors are often greeted by a sea of thick fog or clouds in the morning, especially after it rains.

Hutan Adat Kalong

Hutan Adat Kalong is a valuable ecotourism and historical heritage site in Cipta Karya Tourism Village, Bengkayang. The forest covers an area of about 100 hectares and is a former ancient settlement of the Bakati' Dayak sub-tribe, making it a green site full of meaning.

Its main uniqueness is that Hutan Adat Kalong is a habitat for rare and endemic flora of Kalimantan, including the Rafflesia Tuan Mudae flower. This forest is also a strictly guarded area under customary law, which prohibits logging and only allows the use of certain natural products (such as fallen agarwood).

Bukit Jamur

Named Bukit Jamur (which is said to come from the word "jemur" or "Stone Mountain") because of its rocky and imposing peak, this hill offers a stunning 360-degree view from an altitude of about 500 masl. Its main wonder is the vast sea of thick clouds that often covers the valley in the morning, creating the illusion of being above the world.
