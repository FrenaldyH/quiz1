Explore Bengkayang's Culinary Delights

Bengkayang Regency is home to a rich culinary scene, a unique blend of Dayak and Malay traditions with Chinese influences. Many dishes utilize forest products and fermentation techniques.

Tempoyak

Tempoyak is one of the most popular culinary dishes in Kalimantan, and Bengkayang is known for producing the best durian. Tempoyak is made from fermented durian pulp, creating a unique sour-sweet-savory flavor. It is commonly processed into a spicy and savory chili paste, cooked with fried anchovies, or used as a stir-fry seasoning. It is a must-have side dish during durian season.

Lemang

Lemang is a traditional dish that is very popular and often served during traditional ceremonies or celebrations. It is made from glutinous rice and coconut milk that are put into a bamboo stick lined with banana leaves and then grilled until cooked. It has a savory, fragrant, and dense texture. Lemang is best enjoyed warm with side dishes or simply with Tempoyak.

Pulut Kantong Semar

This is a very unique Dayak culinary dish and an example of local wisdom. Glutinous rice is cooked inside the natural pitcher of a pitcher plant (a type of carnivorous plant) as a substitute for banana leaves or bamboo. The rice is cooked until it is done and has a distinct aroma, often served during traditional ceremonies.

Bubur Pedas

Although it is more known as a Sambas Malay dish, it is easy to find and popular in Bengkayang. Despite its name meaning "spicy," the spiciness is not dominant. The savory taste comes from a mixture of roasted rice, grated coconut, and various forest vegetables such as ferns, star gooseberry leaves, and polygonum leaves (which give it a unique aroma). This porridge is often served with a sprinkle of peanuts and fried anchovies.

Choi Pan

Although more well-known in Pontianak and Singkawang, Choi Pan is also easy to find in Bengkayang. It is a very popular snack. It consists of small cakes filled with vegetables (usually jicama or chives) wrapped in a thin rice flour skin, steamed, and served with a sprinkle of fried garlic and a spicy vinegar dipping sauce.

Bubur Gunting

This is a popular dessert, especially at Chinese food stalls. It is made from hulled green beans and cooked into a thick porridge. It is served with a light sweetness and savory taste, and its texture is smoother and cleaner than regular green bean porridge.
