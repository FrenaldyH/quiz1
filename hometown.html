Bengkayang - West Kalimantan

Bengkayang is a relatively new but charming district in West Kalimantan. Located at the northern tip of the province, it borders directly with Malaysia (Sarawak) and has unique characteristics that make it special.

About Bengkayang

Dayak Traditional Clothes

The traditional Dayak clothes from Bengkayang have distinct characteristics in their materials and ornaments. These clothes not only function as attire but also reflect identity, social status, and spiritual beliefs.

Tatung

Traditional Dayak clothing for men and women differs. Men typically wear a vest made of tree bark, shorts, and a headpiece decorated with hornbill feathers. Women wear a sleeveless top adorned with beads and a skirt made of tree bark. They also accessorize with necklaces, bracelets, and earrings made of beads or bone.

Barape Sawa

Barape' Sawa' is an annual traditional ceremony held by the Bakati' sub-tribe of the Dayak people in Bengkayang Regency. Literally, "Barape' Sawa'" means "paddy harvest festival". This ritual is an expression of gratitude to Jubata (God) for the bountiful paddy harvest.

Mandau

The Mandau is the most famous traditional weapon of the Dayak tribe. More than just a weapon, the Mandau holds immense cultural and spiritual value for the Dayak community. Its shape resembles a machete, but with intricate carvings and ornaments, making it one of the most valuable cultural heritage items.

Nyobeng

Nyobeng is a post-harvest ritual where community elders clean the skulls of their ancestors in the traditional Baluk longhouse as a form of gratitude. This ceremony reflects the deep spiritual beliefs and profound local wisdom of the community.

The border between Indonesia and Malaysia

A Window of Cross-Border Trade This article focuses on the Jagoi Babang Cross-Border Post (PLBN), a vital gateway connecting Indonesia and Malaysia. It showcases Bengkayang's modern face by highlighting the PLBN's role as a center for economic activity and cultural exchange, where people from both countries can interact and trade goods. This provides an interesting geopolitical and economic perspective on Bengkayang.
